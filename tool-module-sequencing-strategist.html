Effective Sequencing Tips

Front-load Foundational Concepts

Start with essential knowledge and core definitions that students need to understand more complex topics later. This reduces cognitive load and builds confidence early.

Build in Review Weeks

Strategically place consolidation or review weeks to help students synthesize information and make connections between modules. Consider every 3-4 weeks.

Align Assessments with Clusters

Group related modules and align major assessments (exams, projects) with these clusters. This reinforces connections and provides clear learning milestones.

Distribute Cognitive Load

Alternate between heavy concept modules and more applied or practical modules. Balance difficulty and pacing to maintain engagement throughout the course.

Show Module Dependencies

Clearly map which modules depend on prior knowledge. This helps students understand the progression and makes it easier to identify prerequisite concepts if struggling.

Consider Scaffolding

Build assessments progressively, starting with lower-order thinking and moving toward application, analysis, and synthesis. Use formative assessments to check understanding.
