Policy & Governance

Institutional policies governing AI use in teaching, learning design, and student assessment at Mays Business School. All policies align with Texas A&M University System regulations.

AI tools may be used to support course design activities including content drafting, rubric development, and learning objective alignment. All AI-generated content must be reviewed, verified for accuracy, and edited by the responsible faculty member or learning designer before being published to students.

  • AI outputs are starting points, not final products
  • Faculty retain full responsibility for course content accuracy
  • AI-generated content must meet Mays accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA)
  • Proprietary or FERPA-protected data must never be entered into public AI tools

Courses that permit or require student use of AI tools must include a clear AI use policy in the syllabus. This statement should specify which tools are allowed, how they may be used, and citation expectations.

  • Include an AI use statement in the syllabus (template available below)
  • Define permitted vs. prohibited uses per assignment
  • Require students to disclose AI use where applicable
  • Provide guidance on proper citation of AI-generated content

AI tools used in course design and delivery must comply with FERPA regulations and Texas A&M data governance standards. No student personally identifiable information (PII) may be processed through AI tools that lack institutional data processing agreements.

  • Do not enter student names, UINs, grades, or email addresses into AI tools
  • Use only institutionally licensed or approved AI platforms for student data
  • Microsoft Copilot (via TAMU license) is the only tool currently approved for use with internal institutional data
  • Contact TLI before integrating any new AI tool that will process student information

Mays Business School supports a balanced approach to academic integrity in the age of AI. Detection tools are one signal among many; they should not be used as the sole basis for academic misconduct findings.

  • Turnitin AI detection is available but produces probabilistic estimates, not certainty
  • Assignment design is the most effective academic integrity strategy
  • Prioritize assessments that require personal reflection, application, or in-class performance
  • Document your AI policy expectations clearly in every assignment description

All AI-generated content published in Canvas or distributed to students must meet the same accessibility standards as any other course material. AI tools do not reliably produce accessible output by default.

  • Check heading structure, alt text, and reading order on all AI-generated documents
  • Verify color contrast ratios meet WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1 for body text)
  • Ensure AI-generated captions and transcripts are edited for accuracy
  • Use the Ally accessibility checker in Canvas before publishing

AI Literacy Framework

A four-level competency model for building AI literacy among faculty and students at Mays. Each level builds on the previous, with clear indicators and practical applications mapped to course design activities.

Level Competency Faculty Application Student Outcome
1 — Awareness Understands what AI is, how it works at a high level, and where it appears in everyday tools. Can distinguish AI-assisted from non-AI processes. Include a brief AI orientation in the Getting Started module. Identify which course tools use AI features (e.g., Grammarly, Canvas recommendations). Students can identify AI-powered features in tools they already use and articulate basic capabilities and limitations.
2 — Application Can use AI tools effectively for defined tasks. Understands prompt engineering basics and can evaluate output quality. Design assignments that permit AI use with clear parameters. Provide prompt templates. Model effective AI use in class demonstrations. Students can use approved AI tools to complete defined tasks, write effective prompts, and assess whether AI output meets assignment requirements.
3 — Evaluation Can critically assess AI outputs for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness. Understands ethical implications and institutional policy. Build in peer-review activities where students evaluate AI-generated work. Include case studies on AI bias in business contexts. Require reflection on AI use. Students can identify errors, biases, and ethical issues in AI outputs. They can justify when AI use is and is not appropriate for a given context.
4 — Integration Can design workflows that strategically incorporate AI to enhance outcomes. Understands organizational and societal impacts. Co-design course projects where students build AI-augmented solutions for real business problems. Integrate AI into capstone and experiential learning. Students can propose, build, and defend AI-integrated solutions within professional and ethical boundaries relevant to their discipline.

AI Integration Workflow

A six-step process for faculty and learning designers to evaluate, plan, and integrate AI tools into a course. This workflow applies whether you are building a new Flex Online course or enhancing an existing on-campus section.

1
Identify the Need

Define the instructional problem or opportunity. What task will AI support: content creation, assessment, student engagement, or workflow efficiency?

2
Check Policy Alignment

Review Mays AI governance policies. Confirm the proposed use complies with FERPA, accessibility standards, and academic integrity requirements.

3
Select Approved Tool

Choose from the approved tool list above. If your use case requires an unlisted tool, submit a tool evaluation request to TLI.

4
Design with Guardrails

Define clear parameters: what AI will and will not do, what the human review process looks like, and how students will be informed.

5
Pilot and Collect Feedback

Test in a single module or assignment. Gather student and instructor feedback before scaling across the full course.

6
Evaluate and Document

Assess impact on learning outcomes and efficiency. Document what worked, what did not, and share findings with TLI for the knowledge base.
