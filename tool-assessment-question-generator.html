Assessment Design Tips

✏️ Write Complete Question Stems

Frame your question stems as complete statements or questions. Avoid starting with "Which of the following" without a complete thought.

X Avoid "All of the Above"

Never use "all of the above" or "none of the above" options. These reduce question validity and can be guessed with partial knowledge.

1 One Concept Per Question

Each question should assess a single learning outcome or concept. Multiple concepts in one question reduce clarity and reliability.

💼 Use Realistic Business Contexts

Ground questions in authentic business scenarios and real-world applications. This increases relevance and reduces memorization-only responses.

🎲 Vary Question Types

Mix question formats to assess different cognitive skills. Essay questions reveal deeper understanding, while multiple choice checks factual knowledge efficiently.

🤖 Design for AI Resilience

Create questions that require analysis, synthesis, or application of concepts rather than simple information retrieval. Context-dependent scenarios are harder for AI to game.

🎯 Map to Learning Objectives

Ensure every question aligns with stated learning objectives. This validates your assessment and helps students understand what they're being evaluated on.

🔍 Review for Ambiguity

Pilot test questions with colleagues or pilot groups. Look for unintended interpretations or trick questions that test test-taking skill rather than content knowledge.
