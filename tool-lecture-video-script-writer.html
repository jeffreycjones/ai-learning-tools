Tips for Best Results

Start With Bullets, Not Paragraphs

The AI writes the flowing prose. Your job is to give it the bones: key points, examples, and the order they should appear.

Match Runtime to Content

A 10-minute video holds roughly 1,200-1,400 spoken words. If your outline has more, either tighten the scope or split into two videos.

Read It Out Loud Before Recording

Scripts that look fine on the page often trip up on camera. Read through once to catch tongue-twisters, long sentences, and awkward transitions.

Visual Cues Are Prompts, Not Instructions

The (Visual:) and [SLIDE:] markers are suggestions for the production team. Learning Media will refine them against available assets and brand standards.

One Concept Per Video

If your draft script has more than one learning objective, consider splitting it. Shorter, single-concept videos perform better for retention and rewatchability.

Hook Earns the First Minute

Open with a question, a concrete problem, or a surprising claim. Faculty introductions and "today we will cover" lists lose viewers in the first 30 seconds.
