Tips for Effective Discussion Prompts

Avoid Yes/No Questions

Frame questions to require explanation and reasoning. Instead of "Is X important?" ask "How does X impact...?" to promote deeper thinking.

Connect to Real-World Application

Link discussion topics to current events, industry trends, or business scenarios your students encounter. Relevance increases engagement and retention.

Build in Peer Interaction

Include prompts that ask students to respond to peers, build on ideas, or constructively challenge viewpoints. This fosters collaborative learning.

Scaffold Complexity Gradually

Start with prompts requiring lower-order thinking and build toward analysis and evaluation. This supports learners at all levels.

Include Grading Guidance

Ask AI to specify what constitutes a strong response. This helps students understand expectations and makes your grading more consistent.

Consider Accessibility

Use inclusive language and ensure prompts don't favor particular backgrounds or experiences. Be mindful of diverse learning needs.
