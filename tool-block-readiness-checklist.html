Block Readiness Checklist

A tailored readiness checklist for Mays 8-week block teaching. Designed around the 1-week overlap between Block A finishing and Block B launching. Built for faculty and TLI designers supporting launch.

About the overlap week: Mays 8-week blocks typically overlap by 1 week. Running back-to-back blocks means finishing one course while launching another in the same week. This is the most demanding moment in your block cadence. This checklist aims to make sure nothing critical drops during that overlap. If you are consistently feeling under-resourced in this rhythm, that is a signal worth raising with your department, not a personal failing.

Tips for Best Results

Run It Early, Run It Again

Generate the full-cycle view 3 weeks before launch, save it, then regenerate for the overlap week to get the compressed version when you need it most.

Back-to-Back Is the Hardest

If you are running consecutive blocks, name your finishing course in the Block A field. The tool will tag items as Block A wind-down vs Block B launch so you can see both streams at once.

Critical vs Recommended

Items marked CRITICAL are the "nothing drops" list. If time collapses, those are what you hold. RECOMMENDED items are the full-quality standard; nice to hit, not catastrophic to miss.

The Tool Does Not Replace Review

The checklist is tailored by AI based on your inputs. It is not an authoritative Mays compliance document. Use it as a working aid, not a sign-off.

Flag What the Tool Cannot See

If you have course-specific items the AI would not know about (new textbook rollout, pilot technology, large enrollment accommodation), put them in Special Considerations so they show up in your output.

Share With Your TLI Designer

Copy the checklist into your TLI consultation or Wrike ticket so your designer can see what you are working through. It speeds up support conversations.
