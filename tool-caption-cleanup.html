Tips for Best Results

More Speaker Detail = Better Attribution

Give the AI a short identifier for each speaker (role, a phrase they say, an acronym they use). Generic names alone are hard to attribute correctly.

Work in Sections for Long Recordings

For recordings over 45 minutes, paste in 15-20 minute chunks. Long transcripts can exceed the model's context and lose coherence mid-way.

Never Publish Without Human Review

WCAG compliance requires accurate captions. AI cleanup is a starting point, not a final product. A human must review before publishing.

Flag, Do Not Guess

If you suspect the source is garbled, enable "Flag Inaudible." Forcing the AI to guess at unclear passages produces plausible but wrong text, which is worse than a clear [inaudible] marker.

Technical Term Cleanup Needs Context

If your recording uses specialized terminology (DCF, CAPM, HR acronyms), note them in Additional Notes. The AI has no way to know "DC F" should be "DCF" without context.

Clean Captions Help Everyone

Clean transcripts are not just for accessibility. They enable search, summarization, and reuse as reading material for students who prefer text. Treat the workflow as a multi-purpose asset, not an accessibility checkbox.
