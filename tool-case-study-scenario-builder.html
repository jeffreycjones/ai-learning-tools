Tips for Effective Case Studies

Ground Cases in Realistic Data

Use authentic financial metrics, market conditions, and industry challenges. Realistic details make cases more engaging and credible for students.

Include Multiple Valid Perspectives

Design cases where different stakeholders have competing interests. This encourages students to consider viewpoints beyond their initial assumptions.

Vary Complexity Across Your Course

Use simpler cases early to build analytical skills, then introduce advanced cases with ambiguous data and competing priorities as students progress.

Connect to Current Business Trends

Reference contemporary challenges like digital transformation, supply chain disruption, sustainability pressures, or post-pandemic business shifts.

Build in Ethical Dimensions

Even business cases benefit from ethical considerations. Cases that require moral reasoning develop judgment and character alongside technical skills.

Test for AI-Resilience

Ensure your cases require judgment, tradeoff analysis, and stakeholder consideration that prevents simple AI-generated answers. Good cases demand synthesis, not summaries.
