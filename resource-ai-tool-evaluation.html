AI Tool Evaluation Checklist

A two-layer instrument for evaluating any AI tool proposed for Mays courses or operations. Pass/fail hard gates screen for non-negotiables. A four-level scored rubric across five domains produces the recommendation that flows through a three-stage governance review.

⬇ Download Mays-branded .docx Preview the governance flow

How to use this checklist

The checklist is the evaluation instrument of record for any AI tool adoption decision at TLI. Use it for new tool requests, vendor renewals, or material changes to an existing tool's functionality or data handling.

Sequencing rule: Hard gates are evaluated first. Any single hard-gate failure terminates the review. Scored rubric is completed only after all hard gates pass.

Three-stage governance flow

Every tool moves through the same three stages. Owners are named, artifacts are required at each gate, and no stage skips.

Stage 1

Designer Pre-Screen

Owner: Assigned Learning Designer

Completes tool metadata, hard-gate section, and a first pass at the scored rubric. Escalates only if hard gates pass and the rubric average is at or above 2.5.

Stage 2

AI Committee Review

Owner: TLI AI Committee

Reviews every tool regardless of score. Verifies hard-gate evidence, validates the designer's rubric scoring, and issues one of: approve, approve with conditions, or decline.

Stage 3

Executive Director Approval

Owner: Executive Director, TLI

Final sign-off. Required for all approvals and conditional approvals. Confirms budget, procurement path, and accountability for review cadence.

Section 1: Hard gates (pass/fail)

Each requirement below must pass. A single failure terminates the review. Evidence is required in every row — an assertion without a linked document or screenshot does not satisfy the gate.

Section 2: Scored rubric (four levels)

Five domains, four criteria each. Each criterion is scored 1 through 4 using the performance levels below. The domain average is reported. An overall average rolls up to the decision matrix.

LevelDefinition
4 — ExemplaryExceeds institutional standard. Sets a positive reference point for future tool decisions.
3 — ProficientMeets institutional standard. Acceptable without reservation.
2 — DevelopingPartially meets standard. Adoption possible only with explicit mitigations in place.
1 — InsufficientDoes not meet standard. Adoption not recommended.

Section 3: Decision matrix

After the rubric is complete, calculate the overall average score across all five domains. Use the matrix to translate the score into a recommended action. The AI Committee is not bound by the matrix but must document any deviation.

ResultRecommended actionConditions
Any hard gate failsDeclineTool cannot be adopted at this time. Document the failing gate and share with vendor. Re-evaluation requires vendor remediation.
Overall average 3.5 – 4.0 and all gates passApproveRoute to Executive Director for final sign-off. Proceed to contract and onboarding.
Overall average 3.0 – 3.4 and all gates passApprove with conditionsAI Committee names the specific gaps, required mitigations, and re-review date. Executive Director signs once conditions are accepted.
Overall average 2.5 – 2.9 and all gates passPilot onlyLimited-scope, time-bounded pilot with explicit success metrics and exit criteria. Full adoption requires a second review at pilot close.
Overall average below 2.5DeclineAdoption not recommended. Revisit only if vendor capability materially changes.

Section 4: Sign-off

Every approval carries three signatures on the record. No skips. No signature blocks left blank.

StageRoleArtifact required
Stage 1 Pre-screenLearning Designer (assigned)Completed metadata, hard-gate evidence, initial rubric scoring.
Stage 2 Committee reviewTLI AI Committee chair and members of recordFinal rubric, decision matrix outcome, committee notes, conditions where applicable.
Stage 3 Final approvalExecutive Director, TLISigned approval memo. Confirms budget, procurement path, and annual review cadence.
Review cadence: All approved tools re-enter this checklist annually, or immediately on any material change to pricing, data handling, or functionality. The designated tool owner maintains the record.
