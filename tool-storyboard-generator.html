Tips for Best Results

Feed It a Finalized Script

Storyboarding before the script is locked wastes effort. Finalize the script first, then storyboard.

Match Visual Density to Runtime

A 5-minute talking head video might have 15-20 shots. A 5-minute animated explainer might have 40+. Production approach drives shot count.

Do Not Skip B-Roll Planning

B-roll is what keeps a 10-minute lecture video watchable. Even a minimal-style video benefits from 2-3 cutaways per section.

Verify Brand Assets Exist

If the storyboard calls for a branded lower third, campus b-roll, or a specific graphic, confirm Learning Media has it before production. Flag gaps early.

Accessibility is Planned, Not Added

Caption-ready pacing, audio-first narration, and on-screen text that does not rely on color alone are planned at the storyboard stage. Retrofitting accessibility after shooting is expensive.

One Video, One Concept

If your storyboard has more than two major section shifts, consider whether the script should split into two shorter videos. Shorter videos hold viewer retention and are easier to update.
