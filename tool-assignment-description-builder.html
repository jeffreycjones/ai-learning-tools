Assignment Design Best Practices

perspective

Write from the Student Perspective

Frame instructions as if speaking directly to students. Use clear, jargon-free language. Explain not just what they need to do, but why it matters to their learning.

lightbulb

Include a "Why This Matters" Section

Help students understand the relevance of the assignment. Connect it to real-world applications, career skills, or course concepts. This increases engagement and motivation.

target

Make Rubric Criteria Observable and Measurable

Avoid vague language like "good" or "excellent." Use specific descriptors that clearly define what different performance levels look like for each criterion.

robot

Address AI Use Explicitly

Be clear about where and how students can use AI tools. Even if AI is prohibited, state this openly. Transparent AI policies reduce confusion and academic integrity issues.

layers

Consider Scaffolding for Complex Assignments

Break large projects into smaller milestones. Provide intermediate check-ins, examples, and templates. This supports student success and reduces overwhelm.

align

Align Rubric Criteria with Learning Objectives

Ensure every learning objective is assessed by at least one rubric criterion. Conversely, each rubric criterion should address a stated learning objective. Alignment matters.
