AI Literacy & Workflow

A competency framework for building AI literacy among faculty and students, plus a step-by-step process for integrating AI tools into your courses.

AI Literacy Framework

A four-level competency model for building AI literacy among faculty and students at Mays. Each level builds on the previous, with clear indicators and practical applications mapped to course design activities.

Level Competency Faculty Application Student Outcome
Level 1
Awareness 		Understands what AI is, how it works at a high level, and where it appears in everyday tools. Can distinguish AI-assisted from non-AI processes. Include a brief AI orientation in the Getting Started module. Identify which course tools use AI features (e.g., Grammarly, Canvas recommendations). Students can identify AI-powered features in tools they already use and articulate basic capabilities and limitations.
Level 2
Application 		Can use AI tools effectively for defined tasks. Understands prompt engineering basics and can evaluate output quality. Design assignments that permit AI use with clear parameters. Provide prompt templates. Model effective AI use in class demonstrations. Students can use approved AI tools to complete defined tasks, write effective prompts, and assess whether AI output meets assignment requirements.
Level 3
Evaluation 		Can critically assess AI outputs for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness. Understands ethical implications and institutional policy. Build in peer-review activities where students evaluate AI-generated work. Include case studies on AI bias in business contexts. Require reflection on AI use. Students can identify errors, biases, and ethical issues in AI outputs. They can justify when AI use is and is not appropriate for a given context.
Level 4
Integration 		Can design workflows that strategically incorporate AI to enhance outcomes. Understands organizational and societal impacts. Co-design course projects where students build AI-augmented solutions for real business problems. Integrate AI into capstone and experiential learning. Students can propose, build, and defend AI-integrated solutions within professional and ethical boundaries relevant to their discipline.

AI Integration Workflow

A six-step process for faculty and learning designers to evaluate, plan, and integrate AI tools into a course. This workflow applies whether you are building a new Flex Online course or enhancing an existing on-campus section.

Identify the Need

Define the instructional problem or opportunity. What task will AI support: content creation, assessment, student engagement, or workflow efficiency?

Check Policy Alignment

Review Mays AI governance policies. Confirm the proposed use complies with FERPA, accessibility standards, and academic integrity requirements.

Select Approved Tool

Choose from the approved tool list. If your use case requires an unlisted tool, submit a tool evaluation request to TLI.

Design with Guardrails

Define clear parameters: what AI will and will not do, what the human review process looks like, and how students will be informed.

Pilot and Collect Feedback

Test in a single module or assignment. Gather student and instructor feedback before scaling across the full course.

Evaluate and Document

Assess impact on learning outcomes and efficiency. Document what worked, what did not, and share findings with TLI for the knowledge base.
